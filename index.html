Welcome to COVIC
COVID-19 Online VIsualization Collection


Why Create COVIC

The COVID-19 Pandemic is touching every part of our interconnected world. Its impact is being felt in all parts of our lives. An enormous number of visualizations designed to communicate, understand, analyze, and predict a constantly changing situation are appearing on the Internet every day, created by our biomedical community, health care systems, governments, news media and the data visualization community at large.

We are researchers at Northeastern University. We want to capture these visualizations related to the COVID-19 as they are happening, before they disappear. We want this collection to become a valuable resource for understanding this rapidly evolving situation. We are collecting any example of visualizations describing:

  • Case numbers
  • Epidemiology models and simulation
  • Economic, social and environmental change
  • Instructions for changing social behavior
  • Advice, resources and tools for data visualizations

In addition to collecting the title and URL, we are adding metadata to each visualization (publisher, date, country, language, type of visualization) that we hope will be useful to anyone engaged in teaching and research. We are collecting visualizations from all parts of the world, created in as many parts of the world, in as many languages as we can find.

You can ADD to COVIC

You can help us by contributing the examples you find. The only *required* information is a valid URL. Please use this Google form to add your contribution to the collection. Include the optional information will help us add valuable metadata.

You can ACCESS COVIC

The collection is being managed in a spreadsheet format. We are adding examples on a daily basis. Coding the attributes of hundreds of examples is an ongoing process. We are happy to share a spreadsheet copy to help the community understand what is happening. We will update and make the work in progress available on a regular basis. Please use this Google form to request a copy of the collection.
